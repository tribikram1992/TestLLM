SECTION 2

Idea Basics

Idea Title
Strategic Workforce Decision Intelligence Platform — Explainable, Graph-Driven Workforce Decisions
Problem Statement in brief
HR data lives in disconnected silos — HRMS, performance, learning, skills, engagement, project systems — with no shared vocabulary connecting them. Because of this, every workforce decision (retention, staffing, mobility, succession, workforce planning) is made on partial data, reactively, and without a traceable "why." Leaders discover attrition after resignation letters arrive, skill gaps after projects stall, and succession risks after key people leave. The problem is not one missing report — it is the absence of a connected decision layer over all HR data.
What you want to achieve?
Build a complete HR decision intelligence platform on three foundations:
  • HR Ontology — a standard vocabulary that maps every workforce entity (employee, role, skill, certification, project, team, learning path) and their relationships into one living knowledge graph, so all HR data becomes connected and queryable.
  • Intelligence modules on that graph — retention & attrition intelligence, skills-gap and readiness analysis, internal talent mobility, and succession/backfill planning. Each module is a different question asked of the same graph, not a separate tool.
  • Decision provenance — every insight, score, and recommendation cites the exact evidence it used, and flows through a built-in decision approval workflow: recommendations are routed to the accountable HR/business approver, actioned only on approval, and logged with approver, rationale, and outcome — making all workforce decisions reproducible, auditable, and explainable.
Attrition prediction is the first demonstrated output — the platform's value is that the same connected foundation answers the next workforce question without rebuilding.
SECTION 3

Business Value

What business value does your solution deliver?
Better decision-making Cost reduction Employee retention improvement Risk & compliance improvement
Describe the expected business impact
One connected platform replaces multiple point tools:
  • Retention — attrition risk surfaced 60–90 days early with root cause, reducing regrettable exits and replacement-hiring costs.
  • Skills & readiness — live view of organizational skill inventory and gaps against current and future role demand, driving targeted upskilling instead of blanket training spend.
  • Internal mobility — employees matched to internal opportunities from the same graph, improving utilization and reducing external hiring.
  • Succession & planning — key-person risk and backfill readiness visible before departures, not after.
  • Governance — every decision carries an audit-ready evidence trail supporting fairness reviews and compliance.
SECTION 4

AI / Technical Details

Briefly explain how AI is used in your solution
AI operates at three layers of the platform:
  • Ontology & ingestion layer — AI-assisted mapping of raw HR data (employee records, skills, certifications, roles, projects, learning history) into a standardized workforce ontology, building and maintaining the knowledge graph automatically as source systems change.
  • Intelligence layer — graph-based models correlate signals across entities: attrition risk scoring with confidence levels (tenure patterns, compensation drift, manager changes, engagement, skill trajectory); skills-gap analysis against role requirements; readiness scoring for mobility and succession.
  • Agentic decision layer — AI agents traverse the graph to answer leader questions in plain language ("why is this team at risk?", "who is ready for this role?", "where are our critical skill gaps?"), explain root causes, and recommend interventions — with every answer citing the exact graph evidence used.
What data will your solution require?
Internal HR data across four categories: core workforce data (employee profiles, org structure, roles, compensation bands); skills data (skill inventories, certifications, learning records, project assignments); retention-relevant signals (tenure, engagement, manager-change history, historical exits for model validation); and role demand data (open positions, role requirements). Includes personal data — handled under role-based access controls and masking (detailed in Section 5).
SECTION 5

Responsible AI / Risk Considerations

Does your idea involve any of the following?
Personal data / PII Bias / fairness considerations
What risks do you foresee with this solution?
  • Employee privacy — predictions rely on personal HR records.
  • Bias / fairness — risk scores could inadvertently correlate with protected attributes.
  • Misuse of predictions — risk scores used punitively rather than supportively.
  • Data quality — stale or incomplete HRIS data producing false positives.
How will you mitigate these risks?
  • Privacy — role-based access controls, data masking, and alignment with organizational data-privacy policy.
  • Fairness — protected attributes excluded from model features; periodic fairness audits on score distributions.
  • Decision approval workflow — no automated action on people; every recommendation is routed to the accountable HR/business approver, executed only on approval, and logged with approver identity, rationale, and any override — creating a complete accountability trail.
  • Explainability by design — every score is traceable to its underlying evidence, so it can be challenged and corrected.
  • Data quality — scheduled sync with source HR systems plus data-freshness checks before scoring.
SECTION 6

Innovation and Differentiation

What makes your idea innovative?
Three things, in combination: (1) an HR ontology as the foundation — a shared vocabulary that connects all workforce data into one graph, which no point solution provides; (2) one graph, many decisions — retention, skills, mobility, and succession are modules over the same connected data, so each new capability costs a fraction of building a new tool; (3) provenance-first AI — every prediction is an explainable path through the graph, and approver decisions and overrides from the decision approval workflow are captured as feedback that improves the system. The "why" is the product, not an afterthought.
Are there existing tools or solutions for this problem? If yes, how is your idea different?
Yes — but they are point solutions: HRIS reports and BI dashboards describe what already happened; standalone attrition predictors output opaque scores; internal job-matching tools handle mobility in isolation. This platform is different in kind, not degree: it builds the connected data foundation (ontology + graph) those tools all lack, then delivers retention, skills intelligence, mobility, and succession as views over that one foundation. Each answer is auditable — reproducible, evidence-cited, and governed through a decision approval workflow. A point tool answers one question; this platform answers the organization's next workforce question without being rebuilt.
